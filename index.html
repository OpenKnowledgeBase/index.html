Process Section - Tree Structure

Step-by-step processes for key topics with icons and a clean design.

Land Resources: Process of Land Use

  • Identify Land Type: Determine if the land is suitable for agriculture, residential, or industrial use.
  • Land Development: Clear forests or natural features to prepare the land.
  • Land Use: Use the land for farming, building cities, or industries.
  • Sustainable Use: Focus on replenishing land resources for future generations.

Minerals: Mining Process

  • Exploration: Survey areas to identify mineral-rich sites.
  • Extraction: Extract minerals using open-pit or underground mining.
  • Processing: Refine minerals into usable forms (e.g., iron ore into iron).
  • Reclamation: Restore the land to its original state after mining.

Soil: Soil Formation Process

  • Parent Material: Breakdown of rocks into smaller particles.
  • Weathering: Natural forces like wind and water break down rocks.
  • Organic Matter: Decomposing plants and animals enrich the soil.
  • Soil Formation: Soil becomes rich enough to support plant life.

Agricultural Crops: Crop Growing Process

  • Land Preparation: Plow, till, and fertilize the soil.
  • Sowing Seeds: Plant seeds at the right depth and spacing.
  • Watering & Care: Provide water, sunlight, and pest protection.
  • Harvesting: Collect mature crops for food or products.

Forests: Forest Regeneration Process

  • Natural Seed Dispersal: Trees release seeds that spread.
  • Germination: Seeds sprout into saplings.
  • Growth: Saplings grow into trees.
  • Mature Forest: Forest supports a rich ecosystem.
